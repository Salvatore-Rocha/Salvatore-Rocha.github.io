        <h2>Teaching</h2>
        <h3>Theoretical Principles for Deep Learning</h3>
        <p>Winter 20, 21, 22 and 23 by <a href="http://mitliagkas.github.io/">Ioannis Mitliagkas</a></p>
        <p>This is an advanced graduate class for students who want to engage in theory-driven deep learning research. Topics: Convex optimization, smooth games, information theory, statistical learning theory. Visit the <a href="http://mitliagkas.github.io/ift6085-dl-theory-class-2020/">course website</a> for the full syllabus.</p>
        
        <h3>Probabilistic Graphical Models</h3>
        <p>Fall 20 and Fall 19 by <a href="http://www.iro.umontreal.ca/~slacoste/">Simon Lacoste-Julien</a></p>
        <p>This course is centered around the formalism of probabilistic graphical models as a tool to encode probability distributions over numerous interacting random variables. Topics: Graphical models: training and inference algorithms, variational inference, exponential families, information theory.</p>
